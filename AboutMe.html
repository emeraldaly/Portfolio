About Me

Internationally Inclined. Curious Mind. Spout of Information. Alarming Laugh.

My journey into web development was kindled when I attended a Startup Weekend Conference in Washington, DC.

At this conference several things were clear:

  1. Developers were a key member of the team
  2. There was abundant funding (free food and corporate sponsors weren't as prevalent in the non-profit realm)
  3. There weren't many women

After I left the conference, I decided I wanted to learn to program. I started to attend tech meetups like:

  • Women who Tech
  • DC Rails Girls
  • DC Nightowls
I took classes like Java Programming, where I learned all about classes and methods. I signed up for tutorials, both low-threshold paid and unpaid. However, I felt I was merely grazing the surface. I started to research Boot Camps to learn more.
